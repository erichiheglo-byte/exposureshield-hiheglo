Frequently Asked Questions

Is a digital will legally binding?
Yes, when properly executed. Our digital wills comply with electronic signature laws (ESIGN Act, UETA) and are recognized in all 50 states. We recommend consulting with an attorney in your jurisdiction for specific requirements.
How do you ensure security?
We use military-grade 256-bit AES encryption with zero-knowledge architecture. Your data is encrypted before leaving your device, and we never have access to your encryption keys. All data is stored in SOC 2 Type II certified data centers.
What happens if ExposureShield goes out of business?
We have a legally-binding continuity plan that ensures your digital will and assets remain accessible. All data is escrowed with an independent third-party trustee, and you'll receive instructions for accessing your encrypted backup in such an event.
Can I update my digital will?
Absolutely. You can update your digital will, asset inventory, beneficiaries, and trustees at any time. All changes are version-controlled with timestamps, and previous versions are archived for legal purposes.
What types of digital assets can I include?
You can include: Social media accounts, email accounts, financial accounts, cryptocurrency wallets, domain names, online businesses, digital content (photos, videos, music), software licenses, intellectual property, loyalty programs, and any other digital property with value.
