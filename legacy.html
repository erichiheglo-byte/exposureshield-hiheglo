Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about digital legacy planning.

What happens to my digital assets if I don't have a plan? +

Without a digital legacy plan, your online accounts and assets may become inaccessible. Most platforms will eventually delete inactive accounts, and cryptocurrencies can be permanently lost if private keys aren't preserved. Your beneficiaries may face lengthy legal processes to gain access.

Is digital inheritance legally binding? +

Yes, when properly structured. Our digital wills are designed to meet legal requirements in most jurisdictions. We recommend consulting with a local estate attorney to ensure compliance with specific state or country laws regarding digital assets.

How do you keep my data secure? +

We use military-grade 256-bit encryption for all stored data. Access requires multi-factor authentication, and we never store unencrypted credentials. All data transfers use TLS 1.3 encryption, and we undergo regular third-party security audits.

Can I update my legacy plan? +

Absolutely. You can update your digital inventory, change beneficiaries, modify transfer conditions, or revoke access at any time. All changes are logged with a complete audit trail, and previous versions are preserved for reference.

What happens if ExposureShield goes out of business? +

We have a comprehensive continuity plan. All encrypted data can be exported at any time, and we maintain escrow agreements with trusted third parties to ensure service continuity or proper data transfer to alternative providers in such scenarios.
